Knosy, an AI meeting companion that keeps working after the meeting ends

Perplexity Agent Build Night · April 2026 · Built in 3 hours · Lead dev and design · Hackathon winner

  • Problem: meetings generate action items and then stall. The notes get written, but no real work starts until someone re-reads them days later.
  • Insight: the expensive gap is not capture, it is the cold start on each task. If the groundwork existed the moment a task was assigned, follow-through would feel almost free.
  • What she built: live transcription (Web Speech API), automatic action-item extraction with assignee detection, and a head-start engine on the Perplexity Sonar API that researches, drafts, and plans each task before its owner opens it. Scoped, designed, and built as lead.
  • Decision: shipped a template fallback for every task type so the demo never failed without an API key. Reliability over raw novelty under a 3-hour clock.
  • Result: won Best Computer-Powered Product. A working live demo, not a mockup.

Hackbubu, a live needs-map that matches surplus resources to local organizations

SF Tech Week Hackathon · November 2025 · Solo build · Developer and designer

  • Problem: people have surplus and the will to give, but not the information: what is needed, where to take it, and when an organization is accepting drop-offs. That gap turns into waste while pantries stay under-served.
  • Insight: generosity is mostly a routing problem. Make the need legible and nearby and the donation follows.
  • What she built: a data-centric prototype on Gemini Canvas with a live map that aggregates real-time requests and lets users filter by category and distance, plus a retention layer (a community heat map and a friends leaderboard) to gamify neighborhood support.
  • Result: pitched live at SF Tech Week with a working proof of concept.

Evergreen Goodwill: strategic waste diversion, owned end to end as PM

Project Manager · March to December 2025 · Team of 5

Her clearest end-to-end PM role: own an ambiguous problem, build the decision system, ship a recommendation leadership acts on.

  • Problem: Evergreen Goodwill sent over 200 tons of unsellable plastic toward landfill each year, with a projected hauling cost climbing past 26K dollars. No structured way existed to compare the flood of diversion vendors.
  • Approach: interviewed 20-plus physical-AI and circularity leaders to map the landscape, then worked with C-suite stakeholders to prioritize the inputs that actually mattered to the decision.
  • What she built: an AI-powered vendor scoring framework that weighed cost, capacity, and proximity, automating a comparison across 30-plus vendors. Managed the team of 5 running evaluations and built interactive KPI dashboards for aftermarket transparency.
  • Result: recommended a single most-viable solution and presented to leadership. 40% faster evaluation, $42K annual benefit, over 85% of plastics placed in end markets.

Blue Shield of California: maternal care equity, research to product

Contract Research Analyst · January to May 2025 · Team of 5 · Mixed methods

Turning 1,200-plus messy data points into a product artifact clinicians could use.

  • Problem: Black women in California face a 36.6% C-section rate against a 31.1% state average. The disparity is driven by systemic barriers, and the qualitative signal was scattered and hard to act on.
  • Approach: designed a mixed-methods study, led 30-plus provider interviews, and benchmarked frontline data against ACOG and CMQCC clinical standards to locate the real gaps.
  • What she built: a centralized coding system that sorted 1,200-plus data points by cause, region, and provider type, which surfaced the patterns behind five structural recommendations, plus a provider one-pager built for actual clinical use.
  • Result: presented to UC Berkeley MPH candidates with an invitation to meet the Dean of Public Health. Recommendations informed expanded budget for maternal mortality prevention and doula training in Northern California.
